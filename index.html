National Overview  ·  Sector Split

Two in Three Schools
Are Government-Run

Government schools account for the clear majority of Australia's schools nationally. Catholic schools form the largest non-government sector, while independent schools — ranging from elite private colleges to small community schools — make up the remainder.

By State  ·  Sector Breakdown

Government Schools Dominate Every State

Across all eight states and territories, government schools make up the majority, but the share of Catholic and independent schools varies significantly by region.

By State  ·  School Type

Primary Schools Lead in Every State and Territory

Primary schools outnumber secondary and combined schools in every state, reflecting Australia's demographic spread. Combined schools — catering to both primary and secondary students — are especially common in smaller states and rural areas where student numbers are too low to sustain separate campuses.

School types: Primary (Foundation–Year 6/7), Secondary (Year 7/8–12), Combined (Foundation–Year 12). Data reflects school campus classifications, not individual enrolments.

Private Schooling  ·  State Comparison

Which States Have the Most Private Schools?

Raw counts favour the biggest states — but when comparing the share of non-government schools, a different picture emerges. Some smaller states have a surprisingly high proportion of Catholic and independent schools relative to their total.

Remoteness  ·  Access & Equity

Most Schools Are in Cities - But Who Serves the Outback?

The vast majority of Australia's schools are concentrated in major cities, yet government schools bear almost all the responsibility for educating students in remote and very remote communities.

Most schools are concentrated in major cities and inner regional areas. Very remote communities have a fraction of the options.

Government schools are the only sector with significant presence in remote and very remote areas. Catholic and independent schools are almost exclusively urban.
